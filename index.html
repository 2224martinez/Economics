Antritrust Laws

The Sherman Antitrust Act
The Sherman Act outlaws "every contract, combination, or conspiracy in restraint of trade," and any "monopolization, attempted monopolization, or conspiracy or combination to monopolize."
Penalties include a $100 million+ fine and can double


The Clayton Act
Has 4 Main Sections
Price Discrimination
Mergers
Director Interlocks
Exclusive Dealings


The Federal Trade Commission Act
Includes all of the other acts above and adds more
This act bans lying to people about what they are getting.

Pros of a Monopoly

Less Wastage
When there is only one producer that means there will be less counterfeits or copies
Not having duplicates means less resources are used to make an identical product


Economies of Scale
When a company can produce more goods their production costs can go down
Lower production costs can then mean the monopoly can choose to lower the price of their product ultimately benefiting the consumer

Cons of a Monopoly

High Prices
They can charge higher because they are the only ones producing that product
The consumer has no choice to but to buy from the only producer


Inferior Goods
Knowing that their product has no demand elasticity, they can produce low quality products
